Regina Paz "Gina" La'O Lopez

An Environmental Activist

Here's the time line of Gina Lopez's Life:

  • 1953 - Born on the 27th of December in Manila, Philippines.
  • 1993 - She produced educational television shows on Science, Math, Values, History and English for elementary and Philippine Literature for high school. For Sineskwela, Ms. Lopez was honored with the UNESCO Kalinga Award, the first Southeast Asian to earn such a distinction.
  • 1994 - She became the Managing Director of the ABS-CBN Foundation.
  • 1997 - She initiated Bantay Bata 163, the country’s first media-based hotline. Bantay Bata was the United Nations Grand Awardee for Excellence besting 187 countries all over the world.
  • 1997 - She established Bantay Kalikasan, for which she received the 1997 International Public Relations Award of Excellence for the Environment and Outstanding Manilans Award for the Environment, 2009.
  • 1997- She was also the Vice-Chairperson of the ABS-CBN Bayan Foundation, which provides microfinance assistance to micro-entrepreneurs. She was also the Chairman Emeritus of the Southeast Asian Children’s Television.
  • 2007 - Lopez also initiated the rehabilitation of the Pasig River and nearby urban streams through the Kapit Bisig para sa Ilog Pasig project. Once considered as the lifeline of the Filipino nation, the Pasig River is one of the most polluted and toxic river systems in the Philippines today.
  • 2010 - For her efforts in rehabilitating the river, she was appointed in by President Benigno Aquino III as the Chairperson of the Pasig River Rehabilitation Commission. Her efforts in the commission led to a river rehabilitation revolution which cleansed numerous tributaries in the Pasig river system. She was also responsible for the reforestation of the La Mesa Watershed Reservation, the last remaining forest zone in Metro Manila.
  • 2008 - She organized Bayanijuan (lit. Country of Juan). Lopez also launched the Save Palawan Movement along with her partner organizations and gathered 7 million signatures for a petition for protection of key biodiversity areas and against mining. Lopez was a vehement anti-mining advocate, known for her stance against large-scale mining in the province of Palawan. She backed the No Mining In Palawan Movement.She also objected to the use of fossil fuels as sources of energy and coal mining.
  • 2016 - Lopez initiated an environmental lecture for Duterte about the need for a better national environmental policy. Duterte then asked her to be his secretary for environment. A few days later, she accepted the offer and was formally appointed by President Rodrigo Duterte to head the Department of Environment and Natural Resources or DENR.
  • 2017 - Lopez hosted an environmental show on ABS-CBN, entitled G Diaries. The show focuses on environmental conservation and innovations. The show also premiered on ANC due to its high ratings.
  • 2019 – She died at the age of 65.
